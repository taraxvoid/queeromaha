# Queer-Supportive Cafes

## Mill on Leavenworth

Large cafe, seating is limited during busy times. Gendered bathrooms with stalls.
[Website](https://millcoffee.com/)

---

## Roast Coffeehouse (Aksarben Village + 114th & Blondo)

Great coffee and pastries, supportive staff and owners. Aksarben location has a single-stall bathroom.
[Website](https://www.roastcoffeehouse.com/)

---

## Urban Abbey (Old Market)

Operated by progressive United Methodists. Has bookstore with a lot of queer literature and varied traditions. Doesn't sell a real lunch. Bathrooms are all-gender.
[Website](https://www.urbanabbeyomaha.com/)

---

## Edge of the Universe Cafe (Benson)

Whimsical, themed cafe in Benson with creative drinks, desserts, and decor. Hosts art pop-ups, music, and unique community events in a playful atmosphere. Bathrooms are single-stall.
[Website](https://www.edgeoftheuniverseomaha.com/)

---

## Legends Comics & Coffee (Benson)

Comic shop and coffee bar in Benson. Queer-friendly staff, hosts game nights, and community events. Good spot for casual hangs and browsing comics.
Features a big queer section with LGBTQ+ comics, graphic novels, and books.
[Website](https://www.legendscomicsandcoffee.com/)

---


## Spielbound Board Game Cafe (Midtown)

Board game cafe with a huge game library, coffee, snacks, and beer. Queer-friendly staff and regular LGBTQ+ game nights. Great for group hangs and casual play.
[Website](https://spielbound.com/)

---


## Blue Line Cafe (Downtown)

Classic Omaha coffee shop in the Old Market, known for its artsy vibe and welcoming atmosphere. Popular with local artists, musicians, and queer folks. Features rotating art shows, comfy seating, and a variety of drinks and snacks. Bathrooms are single-stall and gender-neutral.
[Website](https://www.bluelinecoffee.com/)

---


## Amateur Coffee (Downtown)

Tiny, minimalist coffee shop with a strong queer-friendly vibe. Very little seating—mostly standing room or takeout. Only sells glass containers for to-go coffee (no disposable cups). Great coffee, friendly staff, and a focus on sustainability.
[Website](https://www.amateur.coffee/)

---


## Archetype Coffee (Blackstone & Little Bohemia)

Beloved local coffee roaster and cafe with two locations:
- **Blackstone**: Cozy, modern space with a welcoming vibe. Popular with artists, students, and queer folks. Limited seating, but great for meetups and casual hangs. Single-stall, gender-neutral bathrooms. 3926 Farnam St.
- **Little Bohemia**: Bright, airy shop in a historic building. Community-focused, hosts art pop-ups and events. Single-stall, gender-neutral bathrooms. 1419 S 13th St.
[Website](https://archetypecoffee.com/)

---


## Lucy's Ethiopian Coffee (Downtown)

Unique, family-run Ethiopian coffee shop in the Old Market. Known for its warm hospitality, traditional Ethiopian coffee ceremony, and delicious pastries. Welcoming to all, with a cozy atmosphere and single-stall bathrooms. Great spot for trying something new and supporting a local immigrant-owned business.
[Website](https://lucysethiopiancoffee.com/)

---


## Sweet Magnolias (Downtown)

Bakery in the Old Market specializing in pastries, cookies, and sweets. No coffee and no seating—takeout only. Friendly staff and a great spot for a treat while exploring the area.
[Website](https://sweetmagnoliasbake.com/)

---

## Reading Room Omaha (Downtown)

Cozy, queer-friendly cafe and bookstore in the Old Market. Hosts a regular poetry open mic night and other literary events. Great spot for coffee, browsing books, and connecting with the local creative community. Single-stall, gender-neutral bathrooms.
[Website](https://readingroomomaha.com/events/)
